NICE NG97 Dementia: Assessment, Management and Support

1. COGNITIVE ASSESSMENT INSTRUMENTS

1.1 General Cognitive Screening
- Use standardised cognitive instruments for dementia assessment
- MMSE (Mini-Mental State Examination): 30-point scale, widely validated
- MoCA (Montreal Cognitive Assessment): More sensitive than MMSE for mild cognitive impairment
- ACE-III (Addenbrooke's Cognitive Examination): Comprehensive assessment across multiple domains

1.2 Memory Assessment
- Episodic memory should be assessed using validated tools
- Consider ADAS-Cog for clinical trials and monitoring progression
- Semantic memory assessment important for differential diagnosis

1.3 Executive Function
- Trail Making Test for attention and executive function
- Frontal Assessment Battery (FAB) for frontotemporal dementia

2. FUNCTIONAL ASSESSMENT

2.1 Activities of Daily Living
- ADL (Activities of Daily Living): Basic self-care activities
- IADL (Instrumental Activities of Daily Living): Complex daily tasks
- Assessment should be collateral from family/carers

2.2 Clinical Staging
- CDR (Clinical Dementia Rating): Global staging tool
- Rates cognition and function across 6 domains

3. BEHAVIORAL AND PSYCHOLOGICAL SYMPTOMS

3.1 Depression Screening
- GDS (Geriatric Depression Scale): Validated in dementia populations
- Screen for depression in all dementia assessments

3.2 Neuropsychiatric Symptoms
- NPI (Neuropsychiatric Inventory): Assesses 12 behavioral domains
- Essential for care planning and caregiver support

4. ASSESSMENT PATHWAY

4.1 Initial Assessment
- Comprehensive history from patient and informant
- Cognitive screening using validated instruments
- Functional assessment
- Physical examination including neurological assessment

4.2 Risk Assessment
- Falls risk assessment
- Driving safety evaluation
- Medication review for polypharmacy and interactions
- Safeguarding concerns including self-neglect

4.3 Diagnostic Investigations
- Blood tests to exclude reversible causes
- Neuroimaging (CT or MRI) to exclude structural lesions
- Consider specialized imaging (PET, SPECT) if diagnostic uncertainty

5. QUALITY STANDARDS

5.1 Timely Assessment
- Assessment should be completed within 6 weeks of referral
- Urgent assessment for rapidly progressive symptoms

5.2 Multidisciplinary Approach
- Involve appropriate specialists (neurology, psychiatry, geriatrics)
- Access to neuropsychology for detailed cognitive assessment
- Occupational therapy for functional assessment

5.3 Person-Centered Care
- Involve person with dementia in all decisions
- Assess mental capacity for relevant decisions
- Provide information in accessible formats
- Support for carers and families

6. CONTRAINDICATIONS AND SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

6.1 Sensory Impairments
- Adapt assessments for hearing or visual impairment
- Use appropriate communication aids

6.2 Cultural and Linguistic Factors
- Use culturally appropriate and validated instruments
- Provide interpreters when needed
- Consider educational background when interpreting scores

6.3 Physical Health
- Ensure acute illness managed before cognitive assessment
- Delirium must be excluded or resolved
- Consider impact of medications on cognitive performance
